There’s more than desert.

Arizona has an elevation range of over 12,500 feet.

Plus the largest contiguous stand of ponderosa pines in the world.

Flagstaff averages 100.3 inches of snowfall annually.

Arizona has 128 lakes, including Lakes Mead, Havasu, and Powell.

We gots the weather.

Phoenix boasts 296 days of sunshine each year.

The average winter temperature in Phoenix is 60 degrees.

Affordable living.

Living in Phoenix costs 35% less than San Francisco.
And 45% less than New York City.

Delicious food and brews.

Andrew Zimmern called Pizzeria Bianco “the best pizza ever.”

Four Peaks Brewery produces 38,000 barrels of craft beer each year.

San Tan Brewing Co. is one of OpenTable’s Top 100 Restaurant Bars.

Arizona boasts some of the best Mexican food in the U.S.

Arizona has over 60 wineries and vineyards throughout the state.

Nearly 50 unique food trucks serve the Phoenix area, including favorites Short Leash Hotdogs and Pizza People.

Natural Wonders.

Arizona has the grandest canyon of them all.

Kartchner Caverns features 2.5 miles of pristine cave formations.

Barringer Crater is “the best preserved meteorite crater on Earth.”

The Petrified Forest has plant and animal fossils dating over 200 million years ago.

Top notch universities.

Barrett Honors College at Arizona State University ranked #1 nationally in 2012.

University of Arizona’s Entrepreneurship program ranked #3 nationally in 2011.

Thunderbird School of Global Management ranked #1 International Full-time MBA in 2013.

Northern Arizona University’s Department of Astronomy ranked #2 nationally in 2011.

Inspiration and conversation.

Phoenix Art Museum holds a collection of more than 17,000 pieces.

Phoenix Design Week connects and enlightens the design community.

Developers meet for NodeAZ, Tag Soup, PHX iOS Dev, and PHX Mobile Meetup.

Ignite Phoenix inspires and entertains with 5-minute talks.

Science!

Dwarf planet Pluto was discovered at Lowell Observatory near Flagstaff.

UofA’s Lunar and Planetary Lab launched the Phoenix, the first mission under NASA’s Mars Scout Program.

McMath-Pierce solar telescope near Tucson is the largest in the world.

Biosphere 2 remains the largest closed ecosystem ever created.

We love the game.

The Phoenix Suns have been playing in the valley for 45 years.

The Arizona Cardinals are the oldest continuously run franchise in the NFL.

The Arizona Diamondbacks won the 2001 World Series in their fourth season as a team.

The Arizona Coyotes melt both the ice and our hearts.

Historical Potpourri.

Arizona was founded on Valentine’s Day and is called the “Nation’s Valentine.”

Payson is home to the world’s oldest continuous rodeo, started in the 1880s.

The original London Bridge has been reconstructed in Lake Havasu City.

Arizona has the longest remaining section of Route 66, a total of 157 miles.

Where tech incubates.

Phoenix has promising startups like Local Motors, WebPT, Infusionsoft, Parchment, Axosoft, and Appointment-Plus.

And growing communities and resources like SeedSpot, Gangplank, CoHoots, HeatSync Labs, AZ Tech Beat, and AZ Tech Council.

Tons of sweet perks.

Arizona does not observe Daylight Saving Time.

Disneyland is only a 6-hour drive from Phoenix. Las Vegas, 5 hours.

Arizona hosts 15 Major League Baseball teams for spring training.

Scottsdale offers over 200 miles of Sonoran hiking trails.

You haven’t seen a sunset until you’ve seen an Arizona sunset.

Arizona has 322 golf courses and the weather to use them year-round.

Why Arizona?

Perks aside, the best thing about Arizona is the available room to grow. We’re a young state (the third youngest in our nation) and not until the 1950s did Phoenix truly begin to blossom.

We are establishing ourselves as a place not only for great weather, but for diverse culture, exceptional education, and competitive careers. There’s never been a better time for young people to make an impact.

And really, a place is only as good as the people who live there. So come to Arizona for any of the awesome reasons we listed above, but stay for the community.

Manifesto

WhyAZ's mission is to share why it's awesome to live and work in Arizona. We showcase the natural beauty, diverse climate, and vibrant lifestyle we love. Our ultimate goal is to encourage excited, positive people to choose Arizona as a place to grow roots and to contribute to our community.

We encourage contribution to the site via an open source codebase, hosted on GitHub, and solicitation of feedback via a contact form.

WhyAZ is not beholden to any third party and will not feature advertisements, sponsored content, or discriminatory content.
